Gonzaga NBA Players

Domantas Sabonis

Domantas Sabonis; born May 3, 1996, is a Lithuanian-American professional basketball player for the Sacramento Kings of the NBA and the Lithuanian national team. Son of the Hall of Fame player Arvydas Sabonis, Sabonis is a two-time All-NBA Team member, three-time NBA All-Star, and has led the league in rebounds three times. Sabonis started his career in Spain with Unicaja Málaga. He then moved to the United States and played college basketball for the Gonzaga Bulldogs. Sabonis was then drafted 11th overall in the 2016 NBA draft by the Orlando Magic, Sabonis was traded to the Oklahoma City Thunder on draft night, spending his rookie season with the team before being traded to the Indiana Pacers in 2017. He played five seasons for the Pacers and was named an All-Star twice during his tenure. In 2022, Sabonis was traded to the Sacramento Kings. Here, he was named to his first All-NBA team and third All-Star selection in 2023, he led the Kings to the third seed in the 2023 NBA playoffs and helped them break their NBA record 16-season playoff drought.

Jalen Suggs

Jalen Rashon Suggs (born June 3, 2001) is an American player for the Orlando Magic of the National Basketball Association (NBA). He played college basketball for the Gonzaga Bulldogs. Suggs played for the Gonzaga Bulldogs men's basketball team in the 2020–21 NCAA Division I men's basketball season. As a freshman, he averaged 14.4 points, 5.3 rebounds and 4.5 assists per game and was a consensus second-team All-American. He earned First Team All-West Coast Conference (WCC), Newcomer of the Year, and All-Freshman Team honors. Suggs was selected with the fifth overall pick in the 2021 NBA draft by the Orlando Magic. On March 4, Suggs was ruled out for the remainder of the season after undergoing arthroscopic surgery to removed a cartilage fragment in his left knee. In 35 games for Orlando, he averaged 16.2 points, 4.0 rebounds, and 3.7 assists. Suggs won a gold medal with the United States at the 2017 FIBA Under-16 Americas Championship in Formosa, Argentina.

Chet Holmgren

Chet Thomas Holmgren, born May 1, 2002, is an American professional basketball player for the Oklahoma City Thunder of the NBA. A consensus five-star recruit and the number-one player in the 2021 class, he played college basketball for the Gonzaga Bulldogs. He stands 7 ft 1 in and plays the center and power forward positions. Holmgren was born in Minneapolis, Minnesota. At the conclusion of his freshman season with Gonzaga, Holmgren was named West Coast Conference Defensive Player of the Year and Newcomer of the Year. As a freshman, he averaged 14.1 points, 9.9 rebounds, and 3.7 blocks per game. Holmgren was then selected by the Oklahoma City Thunder with the second overall pick in the 2022 NBA draft, making him the highest-selected draft pick ever taken out of Gonzaga and the highest-drafted pick from the state of Minnesota. On November 18, 2023 Holmgren put up a career-high 36 points along with 10 rebounds. Holmgren represented the United States at the 2021 FIBA Under-19 Basketball World Cup in Latvia, leading his team to a gold medal, and earning tournament MVP honors.
